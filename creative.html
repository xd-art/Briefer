Creative brief example

Background

Creative brief or technical brief at the beginning of development giving information about the main idea and existing materials which the customer already has. Accumulate all necessary information that can be helpful to your executor. The requestor should be competent at the background of the project before starting to set tasks. This way you will save time for both sides and reduce misunderstanding during project execution.

Goals and objectives

Then more clear you objectives and goals, the final result will better. The difference between objectives and goals is that objectives are accurate actions executors take to move closer to the goal. They are specific targets that typically have a time-bound schedule or timeline for completion. Turning from the general to the specific like form goals to objectives you can assign task in professional manner.

Measurable Result

Identify how you will know objectives and goals were accomplished. Write the result which you can measure. It can be quantity and quality of files that you want to get or just fixing quantity and quality of results.

  • Fixing result
  • Getting files
Whatever it may be, describe what the project is, how it works, and why you’re moving forward with it.

Competitors

Nearly all projects are conceived with the conclusion objective of expanding showcase share and creating extra incomes. For competitive business growth in revenue can only come at the cost of competitor market share. It is therefore imperative you identify the competitors, their cos, and pros in order to deliver results that will improve your business. You should include both direct and indirect competitors.

Target Audiance

In many cases, it is most important to understand users who will use your result of work. Your target audience research should consist of users’ age, gender, habits, and preferences. `

Project managment

Project management has evolved so much over the years that it’s spun off into so many different techniques and methodologies. And each interesting methodology has its own set of rules and processes involved.

Here are the top 5 project management techniques:

  • Waterfall: breaks down the software development process into 6 phases; requirements, analysis, design, coding, testing, and acceptance
  • Agile project management: includes the client in the software development process
  • Scrum project management: the most popular subset of Agile project management which involves self-organized Scrum teams
  • GTD (Get Things Done): helps prioritize your team’s work efficiently
  • Critical Path Method: helps the team identifies the most important tasks that need to be completed.

The need to quantify, objectify, segregate and delegate tasks properly and proportionately is of high importance and that’s where the use of modern tools comes in. There’s a lot of project management tools out there, and if you’re new to project management, it can be hard to identify what’s essential, what’s beneficial, and what’s not worth your time.
